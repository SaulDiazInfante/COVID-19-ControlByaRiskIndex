3 Model forumulation

Our formulation without control reads

\[\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} S^{\prime} (t)&= \mu N - \lambda_f S - (\phi + \mu) S + \omega V + \theta R \\ I ^{\prime} (t) &= \lambda_f S + (1 - \sigma) \lambda_f V - (\gamma + \mu ) I \\ V ^{\prime} (t) &= \phi S - (1 - \sigma) \lambda_f V - (\omega + \mu) V \\ R ^{\prime} (t) &= \gamma I - (\theta + \mu) R \\ \frac{dC}{dt} &= \frac{\kappa}{N-I} (1 - C) I^{\prime}(t) \end{aligned} \tag{3.1} \end{equation}\]

With force infection

\[\begin{equation} \lambda_f := \left[ \left( 1 + a \cos \left( \frac{2 \pi}{365} t \right) \beta_0 \right) \right] \frac{I (1 - C)}{N}. \tag{3.2} \end{equation}\]

To describe epidemic protocols about number the size of a gathering— the number of individuals in a meeting—we describe policies assuming that a decision maker choose an strategy each week from a finite and countable set of actions. Thus letting a partition of one according to the risk we define the closed loop policy

\[\begin{equation} u_{\beta} = u_{\beta} (C) := \begin{cases} u_{\beta} ^ 1, & \text{if } \pi_0 \leq C \leq \pi_i \\ \vdots & \vdots \\ u_{\beta} ^ i, & \text{if } \pi_{i-1} \leq C \leq \pi_i \\ \vdots & \vdots \\ u_{\beta} ^ n, & \text{if } C \geq \pi_n \end{cases} \tag{3.3} \end{equation}\]

where

\[\begin{equation} u_{\beta} ^ i \in [0, 1], \qquad \sum_{i=1}^ n u_{\beta} ^ {i} =1 \end{equation}\]

For example, Mexico has been aligned with strategies following the analogy of a traffic light. That is, according to the number of confirmed reported cases and other calculations regarding with the instant reproductive number \(R_t\), the level risk is classified as green, yellow, orange or red. Thus, according to this stratification, the health consult recommends a certain set of protocols.

Hence, in the following we consider the control defined on (3.3) as

\[\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} u_{\beta} (C) &:= u_{\beta} ^ i, \qquad \text{if } \pi_{i-1} \leq C \leq \pi_i \\ i & \in \{ \text{green, yellow, ambar, orange, red}\} \end{aligned} \tag{3.4} \end{equation}\]
